Look, it's you!

What do you see when you look at yourself? Figuratively and physically. Getting to know yourself is one of the most important things one can do, and yet, millions of people struggle with it. Try to reflect upon yourself. Your personality, your actions, your habits, and your behaviors.

Finding it hard to reflect?

Don’t worry, I got you. Let’s start with some general reflection questions.


Do I enjoy my physical state?


Do I spend time with positive people?


How do I feel about myself and my position in life?



You can get additional self-reflection questions here.

Let's Analyze Ourselves

When self reflecting, we often tend to find negative emotions that we tend to harbor. Some of the most common negative feelings, which can be classified as mental health concerns are:

Feeling sad or down

Difficulty concentrating

Excessive fears, worries, or guilt

Prolonged stress

These concerns can range from mild to severe and are described further input depth here. If these concens are ongoing and severe enough to cause frequent stress, it you may want to get a mental health screening to check for mental illnesses. Though you cant be diagnosed for mental illnesses until you see a professional, you can receive an idea on what your feelings might be linked to here and here.

Want help?

Regardless of severity of your mental health concerns, you should reach out to those close to you and share your feelings and build a good support system.

But if you’d like professional guidance with your mental health, there are several different steps you can take detailed below.


Talk to your primary care provider (Steps here )


Ask a professional you know and trust


Ask family or friends for reccomendations on professionals


Go through your insurance company, school, or work



You can also start your hunt for a professional here.

How to Pay

Mental health services do cost money. However, there are plenty of ways to get those services with budget in mind. Some ways to lower the cost of mental health services are:


Find a mental health clinic near you


Utilize a therapy app (ex. TalkSpace)


Find a support group


Look for professionals offering a sliding scale payment option
